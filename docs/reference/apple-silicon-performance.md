# Apple Silicon performance — the M2 Max fact ledger's judgment layer

## Owners

- Bjorn Pagen

## Purpose

Microarchitecture knowledge decays into folklore unless every claim is bound to a regime and kept alive by an executable falsifier — that is this document's thesis, and the Apple M2 Max (Mac14,5) on macOS is the machine it is proved on. The intended reader has finished a performance-aware programming education in the Casey Muratori tradition — they can read disassembly, count instructions against a latency table, size a working set against a cache hierarchy, and run a repetition tester — and now owns this chip, on which a specific and measurable fraction of that training is wrong: the latency tables are for the wrong ISA, the cache model has a level that does not exist here and a line size `sysctl` misreports, the mispredict constant is a curve, and the repetition tester itself changes the answer it measures. The central question is not "what are the numbers" but "why did every prior set of numbers go stale, and what container keeps corrected numbers true." Existing coverage misses this because Apple documents essentially nothing and the public corpus — reverse-engineered tables, side-channel papers, blog microbenchmarks, review deep dives — contradicts itself on basics, and nearly every published claim is regime-free, which is precisely how it decays. The resolution mechanism here is execution, not adjudication: every claim below the level of anecdote carries an id (`m2max.<domain>.<slug>`) in the [bumblebench fact ledger](https://github.com/bjornpagen/bumblebench) — a public, antagonistic microbenchmark suite in which each fact has a falsifier, an experiment that tried to kill it, a disassembly gate, and a stamped verification log — and in its [atlas](https://github.com/bjornpagen/bumblebench/blob/main/ATLAS.md), 39 measured plates on which each fact's tolerance band is drawn over the raw sweep that satisfies it. Those are this document's companion artifacts: the ledger is the canonical fact store and re-verification engine (direction of authority is one-way — the ledger overrules this document, and any statement here without a fact id is visibly unverified), the atlas is the visual proof, and the operator's decision procedures live in the repo beside them. This document is the judgment layer: why regime-free knowledge about this machine dies, which mechanisms actually bind it, and what the ledger's correction record proves about the container.

## DOK 4: Spiky Points of View (SPOVs)

- **Spiky POV 1 — "Your benchmark can train the hardware into lying to you."**
   - **Elaboration:** The repetition tester's contract is that repetition converges on the code's true cost. For branchy code on this core the opposite holds: the branch predictor is a TAGE structure large enough (~44K entries over ~100-bit histories — [arXiv 2411.13900](https://arxiv.org/html/2411.13900v1)) to memorize the outcome sequence of a typical benchmark's inner loop, and it treats the rep loop as a training loop. Repeating one 8K-outcome "random" branch window until the minimum stabilizes speeds identical instructions up 4.6× (`m2max.predict.tage-memorizes-benchmarks`) — so *min-of-reps is a biased estimator*: the tester's core statistic selects the memorized world, and the bias is larger than most effects under study. The same class of failure recurs in the instrument's other joints — the unfenced closing timestamp, the DVFS-contaminated clock — and the insights in "The instrument is part of the experiment" develop each mechanism; the Knowledge Tree (Categories 5 and 9) holds the numbers. Counter-case: for branch-free kernels repetition testing is exactly right, and Casey's own material warns generally about measurement contamination — this SPOV names the specific mechanism, magnitude, and capacity edge on this chip. **Incentive flag:** first-party ledger measurements by this document's author; mitigated by public re-runnability (`branchless_compaction --check`). Falsifier: PMU mispredict counts showing the repeated-window speedup is not predictor-resident, or a rep-tester protocol on fresh-per-rep data reproducing the memorized-world figure.

- **Spiky POV 2 — "There is no generic Apple Silicon cost model — only regime-bound measurements."**
   - **Elaboration:** Every major refutation in this corpus is a claim that was true in one regime and silently false in another: software prefetch is a 3.3–4.0× win on DRAM-tier streams with dependent per-item work and a pure loss on the identical L2-resident stream (`m2max.mem.prefetch-regime`); branchless group probing wins in cache and inverts at DRAM tier (`m2max.probe.window-vs-branchy`); instruction dieting is law for retire-bound loops and inverted for flush-bound walks (`m2max.method.layer-law`). Published literature is regime-free almost by construction — a review measures one configuration and prints one number — so the ledger's schema makes REGIME a required field of every fact, and the atlas draws it: shaded regime bands on every plate, so a reader can see an assertion pass where it is asserted and *stop holding* where it is not. Even the folklore the course installs decays this way: "L1 is 3–4 cycles" was two regimes (addressing modes) wearing one number, and the cache model's missing-level and misreported line size are regime errors, not arithmetic ones — the insights under "The cache model, re-grounded" and "The tier decides" develop this, and Knowledge Tree Categories 1, 6, and 10 hold the inversion tables. Counter-case: some facts are genuinely structural (page size, ROB capacity, port counts) and hold machine-wide — but even those carry toolchain and OS-build stamps in the ledger, because the honest scope of "structural" is one chip on one build; the strongest counter-reading is that a good engineer's intuition transfers anyway, and the ledger's own correction record (Insight 16) is the measured rebuttal. **Incentive flag:** first-party; several regime boundaries here overturned this document's own earlier models. Falsifier: a regime-free cost model for this chip that correctly predicts the sign of the ledger's inversion rows (prefetch, window-vs-branchy, layer law) from a single configuration's numbers.

- **Spiky POV 3 — "The machine's biggest performance levers are outside the instruction table."**
   - **Elaboration:** Instruction counting against a latency table — the course's costing skill — mispredicts this core by integer factors, because the binding constraints are structures no table names. The largest single lever is memory-level parallelism: a dependent DRAM load and the ~33 misses the memory system will run concurrently mean restructuring for independence buys up to 33× (111.9 → 3.39 ns effective per miss, `m2max.mem.miss-lanes`), where the best measured SIMD case in the ledger is 2.65× (`m2max.simd.minmax-universal`). Below that sit the flag-port triad (3 of the 6 integer ALUs, whose scheduler pressure halves sustainable miss lanes), the window hierarchy (the load queue binds 4.3× before the famous ROB), and memory renaming (a store→load round trip is 1 cycle in scalar, ~12 in a q register, 7.5 across a call boundary) — none of which appear in any published latency table, all of which move real kernels by more than the latencies do. The insight groups "The core's real limits" and "Memory is the lever" teach each structure; Knowledge Tree Categories 3, 4, and 7 hold the measurements. Counter-case: dougallj's per-instruction tables remain the correct substrate — this SPOV is about which *structure* binds, and structure sizes are per-generation (M1-inherited figures are flagged in the ledger). **Incentive flag:** first-party. Falsifier: kperf occupancy counters contradicting the measured window knees, or a latency-table-only model predicting the gather MLP collapse.

- **Spiky POV 4 — "On macOS, the operating system is part of the microarchitecture."**
   - **Elaboration:** The course's model machine holds frequency constant, stays on one core, and trusts its timestamp; none of the three assumptions survives here, and each failure is measured and OS-build-stamped. An fsync-heavy loop drives P cores to a 0.43–0.88 GHz floor (`m2max.clock.p-core-deep-floor`) and migrates the thread to E cores for roughly a third of its deep-floor samples (`m2max.clock.floor-e-migration`); a ≥5 ms sleep is a placement lottery; BACKGROUND QoS runs the E cluster at its full clock (E cores are narrow, not slow); and the timestamp the OS itself serves is slide-proof by construction while the raw counter read is not (`m2max.os.commpage-clock-slide-proof`). These are not scheduling trivia: they are terms in the cost model, as large as cache effects, and they change with point releases — which is why every claim in this family carries an OS build stamp and lives under a re-runnable antagonist. The insight group "The OS is in the loop" develops the mechanisms; Knowledge Tree Category 11 holds the numbers. Counter-case: a pinned-frequency lab machine with PMU access dodges much of this — but that is not the machine anyone develops on, and treating the OS as noise rather than mechanism is how the folklore ("background ≈ 1 GHz", "syscalls leave P-core state") got minted in the first place. **Incentive flag:** first-party; the fsync story overturned this document's own earlier account twice. Falsifier: an instrument-light placement trace contradicting the migration fraction, or the DVFS floor failing to reproduce on the stamped build.

## DOK 3: Insights

A course for the named reader, in reading order: first learn why the instrument lies, then re-ground the cache model, then meet the structures that actually bind the core, then the tier logic that decides which medicine works, then the OS's role, and finally the method and its seams.

### The instrument is part of the experiment (→ SPOV 1)

- **Insight 1 — Min-of-reps is a biased estimator for branchy code.** The TAGE predictor's capacity edge sits at 8–16K outcomes per branch PC — squarely inside normal microbenchmark working range — and convergence takes 10²–10³ exposures, which the rep loop obligingly supplies. Taking the best rep then *selects* the trained world; unrolling to more branch PCs buys only one octave, because history, not PC, dominates the index. The discipline that fixes it: fresh data per repetition, or cross-run minima (`m2max.predict.tage-memorizes-benchmarks`).
- **Insight 2 — The closing stamp slides by min(remaining payload latency, scheduler drain), and the OS's own clocks cannot.** The unfenced raw counter read is free but reads early — bounded near ~50 ns on throughput-bound payloads, and recovering up to −99.6% of a µop-poor latency-bound span, whose cost lands on the successor. The fix is architectural, not statistical: macOS's commpage routes every libsystem clock through a self-synchronized register, so `Instant`-class stamps are slide-proof by construction (`m2max.timer.stamp-slide-law`, `m2max.os.commpage-clock-slide-proof`). The 24 MHz tick, not stamp cost, is the real resolution constraint.
- **Insight 3 — The clock is a dependent variable; bracket it or measure fiction.** Co-tenant builds swing P cores between 2.4 and 3.5 GHz and manufactured two fake 2× findings; a fresh process's first second runs in a slow band that defeats min-of-reps positionally, because every rep of the first cell sits inside the window together. The defense is an interleaved serial-ALU proxy — and it must be a non-associative recurrence, because LLVM reassociates constant-multiplier chains into parallel chains and fakes the proxy itself (`m2max.clock.process-start-band`, `m2max.codegen.const-mul-reassociation`).

### The cache model, re-grounded (→ SPOV 2)

- **Insight 4 — There are two line sizes, and `sysctl` reports the wrong one for set arithmetic.** The L1D allocates 64 B lines behind a memory system whose transfer granule is 128 B; `sysctl hw.cachelinesize` reports the outer granule — correct for false-sharing and DRAM economy, wrong by 2× for L1D geometry. Both camps of the published line-size dispute were right at different levels; a behavioral probe is the only arbiter, since `CTR_EL0` faults in userland (`m2max.cache.l1d-line-64`).
- **Insight 5 — The middle of the hierarchy is a ramp, not a tier.** The x86-shaped expectation of an L3 latency shelf fails: for dependent loads there is no SLC latency tier anywhere between L2 decay and DRAM — the cost climbs monotonically as a residency mix (`m2max.cache.slc-behavior`), and the L2 that anchors the ramp is ≈5.5 ns, 3× better than the folklore 15–20 ns (`m2max.cache.l2-latency`). The confound is exact: L2-TLB reach coincides with nominal SLC capacity, so naive sweeps mix page-walk onset into "SLC latency." The SLC's real contributions are bandwidth- and MLP-shaped, not latency-shaped.
- **Insight 6 — Translation is small and cheap here; the hundreds-of-cycles walk is x86 folklore.** A 256-entry DTLB and a 3072-entry unified L2 TLB give 4 MiB and 48 MiB of reach at 16 KiB pages; a DTLB miss that hits L2 TLB costs ~7 cycles, a full walk with resident tables ~35 (`m2max.cache.tlb-miss-cost`). Corollary: any latency measurement past ~48 MiB footprint silently contains walk cost — the ledger's own DRAM pin decomposes into memory plus translation.
- **Insight 7 — A padding rule must name the structure it appeases, or it may be inverted.** "Cache conflicts" without a named indexing structure is folklore: staggering columns by odd 128 B multiples appeased set-aliasing — a non-problem, bounded at 1.55× for lockstep scans (`m2max.cache.set-aliasing-cliff-bounded`) — while enraging the stream-prefetch trackers, a 4.6× problem (`m2max.cache.16k-pitch-aliasing`). One page of pitch padding satisfies both; the dense sweep later showed the poison band ~5× wider than the rule that shipped.

### The core's real limits (→ SPOV 3)

- **Insight 8 — The window is a hierarchy, and the ROB is the ceiling code never touches.** Measured by one instrument: load queue ≈136, scheduler ≈140 structural (~115 effective under real traffic), GPR rename ≈340, ROB ≈590 — so for load-dense code the load queue is the wall, for integer-dense code the rename file, and the classic nop-filler ROB probe measures nothing because nops retire-coalesce (`m2max.core.rob-size` and siblings). Which resource binds is a property of the µop mix, not of the marquee structure size.
- **Insight 9 — Flags are a port-topology resource, and the tax reaches all the way to memory.** Only 3 of the 6 integer ALUs handle flag-setting and flag-consuming ops, so `cmp`/`csel`-dense code hits a ~2.8/cycle ceiling — and because each miss's dependent flag µops wait in that triad's scheduler, a serial flag strand halves sustainable miss lanes (14 of 33; independent strands or NEON compares buy it back — `m2max.core.flag-port-asymmetry`, `m2max.core.flag-strand-mlp`). A gather loop's memory parallelism is a property of its comparison code. No published Apple material states this coupling.
- **Insight 10 — Memory renaming makes the stack free, until the shape breaks it.** A scalar store→load round trip renames to 1.00 cycle — architecturally a memory access, physically a register move (Apple patent 11416254) — so spills are nearly free. The renamer's boundaries are where the cost hides: SLP-merged q-register state costs ~12 cycles per round trip at every offset and alignment, and one `bl/ret` boundary disables renaming even for scalar state (`m2max.core.scalar-memory-rename`, `m2max.codegen.slp-store-forward-trap`). Struct layout of hot state and call granularity must be decided together.

### Memory is the lever; the tier decides (→ SPOVs 3, 2)

- **Insight 11 — MLP is a budget, and spending it is restructuring, not tuning.** Effective cost per miss follows a 1/k hyperbola into the lane floor; a random gather pins the wall exactly (28 lanes × 111 ns — `m2max.mem.gather-wall`), and the levers that matter are the ones that manufacture independent misses: batching, index buffering, prefetch with ~200 ns of lead (`m2max.mem.prefetch-regime`). Against this, SIMD's best case here is small — the course's big lever is the machine's second-biggest.
- **Insight 12 — Below the memory wall, the same medicine is poison.** On an L2-resident stream the hardware already overlaps control-independent misses ~6.7× at batch 1, so batching is width-insensitive, software prefetch is a pure loss, and the only surviving cost class is instructions retired (`m2max.mem.l2-resident-retire-bound`). The decision procedure the course never installs: identify the tier first; below L2-residency remove instructions, above it manufacture independence (`m2max.method.layer-law`).
- **Insight 13 — Residency and composition are in-situ properties; isolation studies mis-rank levers.** Residency is a property of phase *interleaving*, not structure footprint — the same components cost ~3× more once realistic foreign traffic streams between passes, and full prefetch coverage, not batching, recovers it (`m2max.mem.residency-is-interleaving`). Likewise the marginal value of an optimization must be measured *given* the others: the branchless window probe that wins in cache inverts at DRAM tier because its candidate resolution turns a prediction-bridgeable control dependence into an unbridgeable data dependence (`m2max.probe.window-vs-branchy`), and the best pairing in the mixed middle was never shipped because each lever was only ever measured alone.

### The OS is in the loop (→ SPOV 4)

- **Insight 14 — DVFS integrates demand, and recovery is demand-driven: never sleep after I/O.** The governor walks a blocked-duty-cycle staircase down to a floor and sinks further on idle; ~10 µs of spin restores working clocks, while sleeping costs 2.0–2.6× plus an E-core wake lottery on ≥5 ms sleeps (`m2max.clock.spin-recovery`, `m2max.clock.sleep-e-core-lottery`). The fsync floor is deeper than its first measurement — the earlier band was the equilibrium the probes themselves maintained (`m2max.clock.fsync-floor`, `m2max.clock.p-core-deep-floor`).
- **Insight 15 — E cores are narrow, not slow, and placement must be calibrated by physics, never QoS.** Under BACKGROUND QoS the E cluster runs its full ~2.4 GHz — the "background ≈ 1 GHz" belief is dead — and E-core L1 load-to-use is the same ~4 cycles as the P core; the wall-clock gap is clock and width, not clocking policy (`m2max.ecore.alu-clock-ratio`, `m2max.ecore.l1-latency`). Cluster identity is user-space recoverable and physics-signed by an L1-size chase ratio — but never from QoS placement, because an idle machine promotes BACKGROUND threads onto P cores (`m2max.os.cpu-number-clusters`).

### Method and seams (→ all SPOVs)

- **Insight 16 — The correction record is the argument: machinery catches what authors and readers do not.** Seven of this corpus's own beliefs were killed by its own instruments, none by a reader: an inverted pitch-aliasing polarity caught by the antagonist (the same inversion a production implementation had shipped); the fsync placement story dismantled twice by its own follow-up instruments; a QoS cluster calibration that inverts on idle machines; a verify-runner that printed green over a drifted run; a mis-shaped tolerance band; the "mispredict staircase" prose corrected by the dense sweep to a smooth ramp; and the pitch-aliasing poison band measured ~5× wider than the shipped rule. Dense sweeps find the structure between pinned rows — bounds-check overhead in-band at both pinned rows but resurgent between them is the type case (`m2max.codegen.bounds-checks-structural`). What remains honestly open is enumerated, not smoothed over: the AMX blocks are the ledger's one FOLKLORE entry, E-core depth (MLP, ports, predictor) has four facts and no model, two DRIFT rows await a quiet-machine re-run, and live conflicts (the k=8/1 MB tagged-fill bistability, the L1-size store-stream bistability, the 35% code-placement lottery's microarchitectural name, the flag-scheduler figure on Avalanche) each carry a named discriminating experiment — mostly kperf PMU counters. A static document preserves none of this; the executable ledger is the container that does.

### Category 1: Cache hierarchy — geometry, policy, and aliasing (SPOV 2)

Published sources:

- **Source 1: Yu et al., "Timer-less cache side-channel attacks on the Apple M1" (USENIX Security '23; primary, peer-reviewed).** DOK1: L1D 128 KB, 8-way set-associative, 64 B lines, 256 sets, physical index bits 6–13; L2 inclusive of L1 (L2 eviction flushes L1 — the attack primitive); minimal eviction set = 8 addresses per set; pseudo-LRU-consistent replacement; attacks work without any timer by exploiting inclusivity. DOK2: the strongest public source for L1D geometry — the attack construction fails if any parameter is wrong; also establishes L2 inclusivity. Citation: [usenix.org (primary)](https://www.usenix.org/system/files/usenixsecurity23-yu-jiyong.pdf).
- **Source 2: EXAM — "Exploiting exclusive SLC in Apple M-series SoCs" (arXiv 2504.13385; primary preprint).** DOK1: SLC exclusive to CPU L1/L2 (line invalidated in SLC on CPU load) but inclusive to the GPU; M1 SLC 8 MB = 64,000 lines of 128 B; SLC hit ~220 ticks vs ~430 miss; pseudo-random SLC replacement; L1D XOR index folding noted for high pages; inter-cluster and CPU↔GPU occupancy attacks at 92% website-fingerprinting accuracy; cache-masking countermeasure degrades performance <10%. DOK2: establishes the hybrid inclusivity policy and the 128 B granule at SLC level; the exclusivity is a capacity optimization with a measured security price and a measured mitigation price. Citation: [arXiv (primary preprint)](https://arxiv.org/html/2504.13385v1).
- **Source 3: GoFetch — "Breaking constant-time crypto via data memory-dependent prefetchers" (USENIX Security '24; primary).** DOK1: M1/M2/M3 P-cores have a DMP that dereferences *loaded values that resemble pointers*; activation requires in-order-ish streams over pointer-bearing memory; disabling the DMP (DIT bit / core pinning) costs ~30% in affected crypto code. DOK2: the only public specification of when the DMP engages — the input for its performance consequences (Source 12). Citation: [gofetch.fail (primary)](https://gofetch.fail/files/gofetch.pdf).

Regime: single-machine relative verdicts, arms interleaved in one session; sequential sessions get no protection. Meaning: the salvage rule for shared-machine measurement.
- **`m2max.clock.process-start-band`** — a fresh process's first cells run at 1.45–1.97 GHz (~0.4–0.6× settled) for up to ~1 s, and min-of-reps does not defend — every rep of the first cell sits inside the slow window together. Regime: spawns not preceded by cluster-heating demand; a child spawned out of a hot parent starts clean (7/7 at 3.53 GHz). Meaning: the band is DVFS ramp plus initial placement, and it defeats the estimator positionally.

### Category 10: Probe physics, composition, and in-situ behavior (SPOV 2)

The isolation-vs-composition campaign: eight experiments (pre-registered predictions, disassembly-verified, proxy-bracketed) chasing why levers measured alone mis-rank in a real engine. All first-party primary, incentive-flagged; antecedents: [Kulukundis/SwissTable](https://abseil.io/about/design/swisstables), [Ross ICDE'07](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/4221715), [Polychroniou & Ross SIGMOD'15](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2723372.2747645).

- **Source 53: `shadow_composition` — the optimization-composition surface (first-party primary).** DOK1 not owned by a fact id: hash-ahead and window probing are substitutes at hit-rate 0, complements at 25–75% mixes; the never-shipped branchy+hash-ahead pairing dominates the whole mixed middle (7.36 vs shipped 7.63 ns/op at L2; 9.76 vs 11.48 at DRAM; −25% on a zipf dedup replay); an invariant ~2.2 ns/op resolution mispredict at 50% hits that no lever touches. DOK2: composition is regime-dependent and isolation studies systematically mis-rank levers — the marginal value of each optimization must be measured GIVEN the others. Citation: [bumblebench (first-party primary)](https://github.com/bjornpagen/bumblebench).
- **Source 54: `residency_budget` — load factor as an L2-sharing budget (first-party primary).** DOK1: the load × pressure × size surface: optimum 33% at ≤8 MB co-resident pressure → 50% at ≥12 MB, ≤6% shallow inside the 25–50% band, edges lose 15–23%; the window probe's ctrl→key data dependency shows a 3× pressure-slope inversion vs per-slot probing (independently confirming the window-vs-branchy mechanism). DOK2: the shipped 33% validated, with a sizing rule keyed on co-residency; cache pressure prices probe SHAPE more than geometry. Citation: [bumblebench (first-party primary)](https://github.com/bjornpagen/bumblebench).

Fact register:

- **`m2max.probe.window-vs-branchy`** — SWAR window-8 probing beats the branchy per-slot probe 1.44× at hit-rate 0 on cache-resident maps at shipped geometry (4.6× at the discovery geometry) and INVERTS at DRAM tier on hit-bearing mixes — branchy wins 1.4–1.7× — because `rbit/clz` candidate resolution makes the key-load address DATA-dependent on the loaded ctrl word, serializing two miss legs prediction would have fetched in parallel. Regime: sign is a function of (tier × hit rate); boundary ≈ the L2/SLC edge. Meaning: branchless group probing à la SwissTable is an in-cache optimization on wide-OoO cores.
- **`m2max.probe.hash-ahead-regime`** — hash-ahead (computing hash(i+1) before probe(i)'s branches resolve) recovers 56–65% of mispredict-exposed hash latency on branchy probes (22.3 → 14.7 ns against an 8.6 ns prehash bound) and is a +1.1–2.4 ns/row pure cost under the window probe — including the mixed dedup path the engine kept it for (the gravestone: the lever was reverted where measured and kept where it obeyed the same law unmeasured). Regime: the lever's sign is a function of probe shape. Meaning: pipelining pays against flush shadows; delete the flushes and only its ceremony remains.
- **`m2max.probe.miss-load-law`** — open-addressing miss cost falls 4.5× (9.6 → 2.1 ns/probe) as load factor drops 38% → 5% on the branchy tagged probe. Regime: branchy per-slot walks, random miss streams, map beyond L1; mechanism: at low load the exit branch is ~95% biased and predicts, and the next probe's speculated hash chain survives. Meaning: a miss-heavy structure buys a low load factor before any other lever.
- **`m2max.probe.pass-overhead`** — the fixed per-pass overhead of a faithfully-shaped batched probe pass is 11–30 ns — 20× below the 200–500 ns the campaign designed against — so all per-pass amortization levers are ~1% effects, with diminishing returns past batch means of ~3–8, not ~30. Regime: presized hot path; a hot-path allocation would be a different, bigger number. Meaning: pin fixed-overhead hypotheses by measurement before building levers on them.
- **`m2max.probe.bucket8-occupancy-invariant`** — a bucket-of-8 layout with a NEON 8-key sweep probes at ≈3.5 ns flat across hit rate 10–90% AND occupancy 0.15–0.4, executing 2.5× more instructions than the dieted scalar probe (IPC 0.8 → 4.1) and halving its time at mixed rates. Regime: L2-resident maps; the same layout with a branchy candidate loop collapses at hit 50%. Meaning: the probe-walk layer is a mispredict-serialization problem, not an instruction-count problem.
- **`m2max.probe.tag-gate-beats-sweep-in-situ`** — under displacement, tag-gated key loads beat full-key NEON sweeps because the sweep touches ≈2× the cache lines per miss; the sweep's isolation 2.7× win inverted to +4.4–25% in-engine regressions and was not shipped. Regime: displaced/missing probe streams; resident maps belong to the occupancy-invariant fact — two sides of one law, split by residency. Meaning: "buy instructions to delete branches" is valid only while the touched-lines count does not grow.
- **`m2max.probe.const-arity-tax`** — runtime-arity key handling taxes the hot dedup insert 1.10–1.50× over a const-generic monomorph even in dieted form (1.28–1.38 at the displaced 16 MB point); the engine's full runtime shape pays 1.7–2.1×. Regime: SWAR dedup insert, ratio between equally displaced loops. Meaning: monomorphize hot-map arity — LLVM performs prefix-hash hoisting and gather-hash fusion for free under const arity, making hand fusion redundant.
- **`m2max.mem.l2-resident-retire-bound`** — an L2-resident probe stream carrying real executor work is retire-bound at batch 1: batching buys 4–9% (−0.8% in the production system that motivated the experiment); the tight-loop boundary case is −31%, still nowhere near the DRAM-tier textbook win. Regime: L2-resident, independent probes, instruction count dominating residual latency; the null IS the fact. Meaning: the hardware already overlaps control-independent L2 misses — below the wall, remove instructions.
- **`m2max.mem.residency-is-interleaving`** — residency is a property of phase interleaving, not footprint: an 8.9 MB map "resident" in a 16 MB L2 runs its probe phase 2.6× slower once realistic foreign traffic streams between passes (13.5 → 34.7 ns/probe), and a covering phase-1.5 prefetch pass takes it back to 11.4–12.1 ns/probe at EVERY tier. Regime: phased executors whose passes interleave foreign traffic; isolated microbenchmarks never show it — that is the point. Meaning: component attribution is context-dependent by ~3×, so single-regime subtraction attribution is systematically wrong for the other regime.
- **`m2max.method.layer-law`** — retire-bound loops diet instructions; flush/latency-bound walks buy instructions to delete branches; the buying side is valid only while touched lines do not grow. Regime: the law's variable IS the regime (what binds: retire, flush, or lines); its atlas plate sweeps the duplicate mix and shows the window-vs-branchy ranking flip near ~52% duplicates. Meaning: a performance doctrine on this machine is a statement about which resource binds, not about code style.

### Category 11: Clocks, DVFS, placement, and the E cluster (SPOV 4)

Published sources:

- **Source 55: Eclectic Light Co — core-management series (secondary; background).** DOK2: the accessible description of macOS QoS-steered scheduling across P/E clusters — the policy layer whose measured physics the fact register pins. Citation: [eclecticlight.co (secondary)](https://eclecticlight.co).
- **Source 56: bumblebench `fsync_dvfs` + `floor_identity` (first-party primary; the fsync-floor resolution).** DOK1 not owned by a fact id: the governor integrates blocked duty cycle — +35 µs of CPU per fsync raises the equilibrium to 2.4–2.9 GHz, and co-tenant load can hold the cluster at 3.3 GHz through a burst; plain µs-scale `fsync(2)` costs only 1–2 DVFS steps; instrument caveats: the serial-mul proxy under-reads E-cores ~17%, and dense 6-wide add streams run ~18% below serial-measured ambient clock at ≥3.0 GHz. The two-instrument story: `fsync_dvfs`'s "never migrates, 176/176 P-wakes" was the equilibrium its own ~35 µs/fsync identity probes maintained; `floor_identity`, probing after a full 48-deep walk with two concordant identity signals (direct cpu-id + L1-size chase ratio, 0.88–0.93 agreement), found both migration and a deeper P floor. DOK2: the first published-anywhere numbers for Apple Silicon post-syscall DVFS state — and the corpus's clearest case of machinery correcting its own prior account, twice. **Incentive flag:** first-party. Citation: [bumblebench (first-party primary)](https://github.com/bjornpagen/bumblebench).

Fact register:

- **`m2max.clock.fsync-floor`** — back-to-back F_FULLFSYNCs walk the issuing P-core down ~1–2 DVFS steps per ~4 ms fsync to a 1.05–1.46 GHz equilibrium floor (over ~10–20 fsyncs of ~99%-blocked duty cycle); recovery is demand-driven, never automatic — idle sinks the governor further. Regime: quiet machine, back-to-back fullfsyncs; anything keeping the run queue busy raises the equilibrium. Meaning: the floor is an equilibrium of demand, which is why probing it changes it.
- **`m2max.clock.p-core-deep-floor`** — cpu-id-confirmed P-core samples under the storm read 0.43–0.88 GHz with P-shaped caches — well below the earlier band, which was the equilibrium its own probes maintained. Regime: UI-QoS storm, torn samples excluded, effective clock via a dependent xor-shift-mul chain. Meaning: the true P floor is deeper than any instrument-heavy protocol can see.
- **`m2max.clock.floor-e-migration`** — the fsync'ing thread does NOT stay on P: ~30% of deep-band (≤1.2 GHz) and ~56% of mid-band untorn samples land on E-cluster cores, with 0.88–0.93 concordance between the direct cpu-id and cache-ratio identity signals. Regime: UI QoS, sustained storm, shared-machine ambient; placement is a scheduler distribution, asserted as a wide presence band, never a point. Meaning: both halves of the prior "stays on one P core" story were instrument artifacts.
- **`m2max.clock.sleep-e-core-lottery`** — sleeping ≥5 ms then measuring is a lottery: the timer wake lands on an E core 25–40% of the time and on a down-stepped P core otherwise, so sleep-then-measure reads ≥1.5× slower than the pre-sleep clock in a large fraction of reps. Regime: default-QoS, pure `nanosleep` — no I/O required; distributional, asserted as a rate. Meaning: the wake, not the sleep, prices the measurement.
- **`m2max.clock.spin-recovery`** — recovery is demand-driven and fast under continuous spin: ~10 µs of spin after the last fsync already reads ~2.9 GHz; sustained ≥3.0 GHz arrives at 8 µs–4.4 ms; the full 3.4+ GHz staircase completes at 9–26 ms; idle recovers nothing. Regime: continuous demand from the wake onward, quiet machine. Meaning: spin, never sleep, before a timed region.
- **`m2max.ecore.alu-clock-ratio`** — under BACKGROUND QoS a serial ALU chain runs 1.39–1.45× slower wall-clock than under USER_INTERACTIVE: the E cluster executes at its full ~2.40 GHz hardware clock, not a punitive efficiency floor. Regime: QoS-steered placement, mode-asserted; measurement trap paid for once — a UI thread blocked in `pthread_join` donates priority through the turnstile and drags the BG worker onto a P core (publish through an atomic, join after). Meaning: the "background ≈ 1 GHz" belief is dead.
- **`m2max.ecore.l1-latency`** — E-core L1D load-to-use is 1.71–1.75 ns ≈ 4.1 cycles at its 2.40 GHz clock — the SAME cycle count as the P core on the identical kernel (1.19 ns ≈ 4.0 cy at 3.3 GHz). Regime: 16 KiB Sattolo chase, L1-resident on both core kinds (E L1D is 64 KiB). Meaning: the E-core L1 is slower only by clock, not by design — E cores are narrow, not slow.
- **`m2max.os.cpu-number-clusters`** — cluster membership is user-space recoverable: `pthread_cpu_number_np` ids are stable, the E cluster is numbered FIRST (ids 0–3; P ids 4–11), and each id can be physics-signed by its L1D-size signature (96-vs-32 KiB chase ratio ≥2 ⇒ E). Regime: macOS 15.7.7; the mapping must be signed by physics, never QoS — an idle machine promotes BACKGROUND threads onto P cores (the first draft calibrated from QoS and got the mapping inverted; kept as a method gravestone). Meaning: placement truth requires a physical signature.
