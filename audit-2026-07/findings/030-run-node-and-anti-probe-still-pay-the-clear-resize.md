## run_node and anti_probe still pay the clear+resize re-memset whose removal probe_pass's grow_scratch already recorded as an owed followup

category: incoherence | severity: medium | verdict: CONFIRMED | finder: engine:run
outcome: fixed 0f13feff

### Summary

Commit cfa480ce ("perf: the redundant zero-fills retired at the owned sites — grow-only scratch") converted `probe_pass`'s seven scratch windows from `clear()` + `resize(n, 0)` to grow-only sizing (`grow_scratch`, probe_pass.rs:595-606), because the clear+resize pair re-memsets the full window every pass even though every element of `[..n]` is written before it is read. The commit's own profile found that the majority of the meets_chain `_platform_memset` cost (71 of 77 samples) sat at **run_node's** mirror sites, measured the post-conversion residual at **54/1498 = 3.6% of meets_chain, "every remaining sample at run_node's own resize sites"**, and recorded the run_node conversion as an explicit followup: "arm the same grow-only shape there consciously, never silently." That followup never landed: `grow_scratch` is used only in probe_pass.rs (grep-verified), TODO.md carries neither the item nor a refusal gravestone, and every scratch window in run_node.rs and anti_probe.rs still uses clear+resize. Worse, `anti_probe.rs:232`'s trailing `hashes.clear()` — the exact pattern cfa480ce deleted from probe_pass's sibling loop — resets the shared `scratch.hashes` length to zero, so probe_pass's own `grow_scratch(&mut scratch.hashes, n)` (probe_pass.rs:82) re-memsets from zero on the next pass of any anti-probe-carrying node, silently defeating the high-water contract it sits next to.

This is a doctrine violation by the code's own words: run_node.rs:165-173 and probe_pass.rs:47-51 declare the two passes line-parallel twins ("a change here needs its mirror there" — the recorded extraction refusal), and the grow-only change went to one twin only.

Doctrine cross-check (docs/design/representation-first.md lens): the pooled high-water buffer IS the representation fix — the per-pass re-zero is control-flow work a better-sized representation erases; the codebase already ruled this way in cfa480ce and the ruling was applied to one of two declared twins.

### Bench impact

meets_chain-class multi-node plans: the leaf pass re-memsets hashes + mask (+ allen_gather where present) per sibling/residual pass per batch per surviving parent — directly measured at 3.6% of meets_chain at run_node's resize sites in cfa480ce's post-fix profile, still unconverted at HEAD. Single-node plans pay the same sites on every batch. Anti-probe-carrying nodes additionally lose probe_pass's hashes high-water every pass via anti_probe.rs:232.

### Suggested fix

Hoist `grow_scratch` from probe_pass.rs to the `run` module root and replace every clear+resize pair in run_node.rs and anti_probe.rs; delete the trailing `hashes.clear()` at anti_probe.rs:232. Three consumers need slicing added during the conversion (the finder's "slicing is already in place everywhere" overstates slightly): run_node.rs:241 `for &hash in &scratch.hashes` must become `&scratch.hashes[..n]`; anti_probe.rs:183 `for &hash in hashes.iter()` likewise `[..n]`; run_node.rs:338 must split `scratch.allen_gather[..4 * n]` as probe_pass.rs:307 already does (otherwise a grown vec makes `b_ends` longer than `n` and trips allen.rs's equal-length debug asserts). run_node.rs:365's full-vec mask iteration is safe as-is only because `allen_filter_batch` resizes the mask to exactly `n` (kernel/allen.rs:120-123); slicing it `[..n]` anyway (as probe_pass.rs:330 does) keeps the twins line-parallel. Re-run the meets_chain interleaved A/B per the cfa480ce protocol to confirm the recovered share.
