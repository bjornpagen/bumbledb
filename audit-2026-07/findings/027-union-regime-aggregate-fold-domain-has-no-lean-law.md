## Union-regime aggregate fold domain has no Lean law; docs and Bridge cite `union_regime_head_projection` beyond its statement; OR+aggregate coarsening is unpinned and the oracles disagree

category: incoherence | severity: high | verdict: CONFIRMED | finder: r2:lean-unswept-modules
outcome: fixed 5a8f89c0 + 9c2aeca7 (R2 made it mandatory)

### Summary

The normative aggregate denotation in the Lean estate is single-rule only: `def aggAnswers (C : Classify) (r : Rule) …` (`lean/Bumbledb/Query/Aggregates.lean:1569`). The multi-rule aggregate semantics — fold domain = the union of the rules' head-projected binding sets — exists only as unproved executable glue in the conformance evaluator (`lean/Bumbledb/Conformance.lean`: `headRow` at 708, `projectUnionGroup` at 740, `evalUnion` at 782-796; the file contains **zero theorems**, and nothing else in the Lean tree references these definitions). Yet the architecture docs and the Bridge ledger cite `union_regime_head_projection` as the law for the aggregate fold domain, and that theorem cannot state the claim: its statement is over `(rule e).finds.map (bind e)` where `Rule.finds : List VarId` (`lean/Bumbledb/Query/Syntax.lean:233`) — pure projection heads, target set `queryAnswers` — so an aggregate head is unrepresentable in it. The theorem's own docstring (Dedup.lean:571-579) quotes the aggregate reading, embedding the overreach in the Lean source itself.

The unpinned semantics is live and behavior-changing: the engine selects `SinkProgram::Union` whenever `rules.len() > 1` (`crates/bumbledb/src/api/prepared/build.rs:131-137`), and those rules are post-DNF (`normalize` runs at build.rs:75-77; `ir/normalize/dnf.rs` distributes every `Or` into rules regardless of head shape; validation refuses only Arg-restriction across lowered rules, `ir/validate/validate.rs:399-415` — Sum/Count/Min/Max pass). So adding an OR condition to a single-rule `Sum(amount) by account` query silently switches the fold domain from distinct full bindings to distinct head-projected `(group, input)` rows. The engine's own test pins the coarsened answer: Sum = 75, "NOT the per-rule sums 35 + 65 = 100" (`crates/bumbledb/src/api/prepared/tests/rules.rs:218-222`).

Verification found an aggravation beyond the original finding: **the engine and both oracles disagree on this input class, and no suite ever generates it.** The naive model dispatches a written single rule to its single-rule path (`crates/bumbledb-bench/src/naive/query.rs`, `if let [rule] = rules`), evaluating the `Or` tree naively over distinct full bindings; the Lean conformance evaluator does the same (`Conformance.lean` `evalQuery`: `| [r] => … evalSingle`). Both would answer Sum = 200 for two same-amount postings where the engine answers 100. The differential generator never emits `Or` ("The tree grammar's OR shapes are the DNF property suite's territory … never the generator's", `crates/bumbledb-bench/src/querygen.rs:176-186`), and that DNF property suite covers projection heads only (`crates/bumbledb-bench/src/naive/tests/dnf.rs:133`, `"no aggregates: no overflow"`). The DNF contract stated in both `dnf.rs`'s module doc and `dnf_preserves_denotation`'s docstring — "lowering-then-evaluating ≡ evaluating the tree naively" (`lean/Bumbledb/Query/Denotation.lean:737-746`) — is proved and tested only at the binding/projection level, and is **false** for aggregate heads under the engine's union regime.

### Failure scenario

Take a passing single-rule query `Sum(amount) by account` (fold domain = distinct full bindings; two postings of 100 → Sum 200, per the doc's own example). Add any OR condition, e.g. `flagged == true | flagged == false`. DNF mints two rules; validation accepts (only Arg is refused across rules); the sink flips to the union regime; the two postings' head projections `(account, 100)` collide and fold once — Sum silently drops to 100. The naive model and the Lean conformance evaluator would both say 200 for the query as written, but no generator produces OR+aggregate cases, so the differential and conformance gates stay green; the Lean census stays green because the Bridge row points at a theorem that is true — about projections.

### Suggested fix

1. State the multi-rule aggregate denotation in Lean (an `aggAnswers`-analogue over a rule list whose fold domain is the deduplicated union of head-projected rows) and either prove `evalUnion` sound against it, prove the engine's regime choice against it, or record the explicit countermodel showing DNF lowering is **not** aggregate-answer-preserving (the honest form of the current situation — the `dnf_preserves_denotation` docstring's "lowering-then-evaluating ≡ evaluating the tree naively" needs an aggregate-head carve-out either way).
2. Resolve the oracle divergence deliberately: either the naive model and `evalQuery` should lower ORs before dispatching aggregate heads (making the union regime the definition), or the engine's regime flip should key on *written* rules, not lowered ones. Add an OR+aggregate case class to the differential generator and the conformance corpus so the chosen semantics is actually gated.
3. Until then, correct `docs/architecture/20-query-ir.md:286`, `docs/architecture/40-execution.md:341`, and the Bridge row so they claim only what `union_regime_head_projection` states (dedup keys of the head-projected **projection** stream), and trim the theorem's docstring quote of the aggregates sentence.
